The Double Headed Snake

The body is the positive real line and the two heads are the points \(0'\) and \(0''\). Definition The Double Headed Snake is the topological space on the set \(\R_{+00}\) of all positive real numbers together with the numbers \(0'\) and \(0''\) that is generated by the basis \(\B\) containing sets of the form \((a,b)\), \((0,b) \cup \{0'\}\), and \((0,b) \cup \{0''\}\) where \(a,b \in \mathbb{R}\).
