Order of an Element in a Group

Definition Let \((G, \cdot)\) be a group and let \(g \in G\). The order of \(g\), denoted \(\vert g \vert\) is the smallest positive integer \(n\) such that \(g^n = e\), the identity element of \(G\). If such an \(n\) exists, \(g\) has finite order. Otherwise, \(g\) has infinite order. Properties By Lagrange’s Theorem, the order of \(g\) always divides the order of the group, \(\vert G \vert\). Subgroups

Definition A subgroup of a group \((G,\cdot)\) is a subset \(H \subset G\) such that \((H, \cdot)\) is a group under the same operation as \(G\). We write \(H < G\) if \(H\) is a subgroup of \(G\). Properties The sets \(\{e\}\) and \(G\) are subgroups of \(G\) for all groups \(G\).
