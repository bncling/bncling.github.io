Invertibility of linear maps

Definitions Let \(V\) and \(W\) be vector spaces. A linear map \(T \in \Lin (V, W)\) is called invertible if there exists a map \(S \in \Lin(W, V)\) such that the products \(ST\) and \(TS\) are the identity maps on \(V\) and \(W\), respectively. We call the map \(S\) the inverse of \(T\), denoted \(T\inv\) (see the uniqueness property below).
