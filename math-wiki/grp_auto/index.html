Group automorphisms

December 22, 2021

Definition

An automorphism of a group \((G, \cdot)\) is a map \(f: G \to G\) that is an ismorphism.

An inner automorphism of \(G\) is an automorphism \(i_g: G \to G\) given by conjugation: \(i_g (x) = gxg\inv\).

Properties

Because automorphisms are bijections from the set \(G\) to itself, the set of all automorphisms, denoted \(\Aut (G)\), is a subset of \(\Sym(G)\), the symmetric group on \(G\). In fact, \(\Aut(G)\) is a subgroup of \(\Sym(G)\).

The set of inner automorphisms, \(\inn(G)\), is a subgroup of \(\Aut(G)\) and is isomorphic to the quotient group \(G / Z(G)\) where \(Z(G)\) is the center of \(G\).

Examples

Let \(f: \Z \to \Z\) be an automorphism of the integers under addition. The group \((\Z, +)\) is cyclic, so the map \(f\) is entirely determined by where \(f\) sends the element \(1\), since \(\Z = \langle 1 \rangle\). Since \(f\) is an isomorphism, \(f(1)\) must generate the group in the codomain, which is also \(\Z\), meaning \(f(1) = \pm 1\). Therefore there are two possibilities for \(f\), one in which \(f(n) = n\) and one in which \(f(n) = -n\), and so \(\Aut(\Z)\) has just two elements, meaning \(\Aut(\Z) \cong\) \(\Z_2\).

We have \(\Aut(\Z_n) \cong U(n)\), the group of units mod \(n\).
