The product topology

July 17, 2021

Definitely review the page on infinite cartesian products for this one.

Definition

Let \(\{(X_\alpha, \T_\alpha)\}_{\alpha \in \lambda}\) be a collection of topological spaces. We can define a topology on the product \(\prod_{\alpha \in \lambda} X_\alpha\) generated by the subbasis consisting of sets of the form \(\pi_\beta\inv (U_\beta)\) where \(\pi_\beta\) is a projection function, \(\beta \in \lambda\), and \(U_\beta\) is open in the space \((X_\beta, \T_\beta)\). We call this topology the product topology.

Properties

Theorem 3.37: The product topology on \(\prod_{\alpha \in \lambda} X_\alpha\) has a basis consisting of sets of the form \(\prod_{\alpha \in \lambda} U_\alpha\), where \(U_\alpha\) is open in \((X_\alpha, \T_\alpha)\) for all \(\alpha \in \lambda\) and \(U_\alpha = X_\alpha\) for all but finitely many \(\alpha\). (This is why the product topology is also sometimes called the finite gate topology).

As a consequence of the above theorem, if the collection \(\{(X_\alpha, \T_\alpha)\}_{\alpha \in \lambda}\) is finite, the product topology on \(\prod_{\alpha \in \lambda} X_\alpha\) is the same as the box topology.

Separation properties

  • \(T_1\)-space (since it’s Hausdorff)
  • Hausdorff (proof)
  • Regular (proof) and a \(T_3\)-space.

Where we are considering the product topology on \(\prod_{\alpha \in \lambda} X_\alpha\) and \(\{(X_\alpha, \T_\alpha)\}_{\alpha \in \lambda}\) is a collection of topological spaces satisfying a particular property. That is, the product of Hausdorff spaces is Hausdorff, for example.

Examples

Theorem 3.36: The product topology on \(\R^2\) is the same as the standard topology on \(\R^2\).

Open sets in the product topology on \(2^\N = \prod_{n \in \N} \{0,1\}\) contain infinitely many binary sequences, so if \(p \in 2^\N\) is a binary sequence, then \((U - \{p\}) \cap 2^\N \neq \emptyset\) for all open sets \(U\) containing \(p\), meaning that \(p\) is a limit point of \(2^\N\) and is therefore not an isolated point. Since no point is isolated, this topology is not the discrete topology, which means this is an example of a space for which the product topology differs from the box topology.
