Sequences

July 17, 2021

They’re like lists but they’re infinitely long.

Definitions

For real numbers, a sequence is a function \(f: \mathbb{N} \to \mathbb{R}\), where the image of the number \(n\) under \(f\) is typically called \(a_n\). The sequence is written by listing its images: \(a_1, a_2, \dots\) or for short, \(\{a_n\}\).

In a topological space \((X, \mathcal{T})\), a sequence is a function \(f: \mathbb{N} \to X\), where the image of the number \(i\) under \(f\) is typically called \(x_i\). The sequence is written by listing its images: \(x_1, x_2, \dots\) or for short, \((x_i)_{i \in \mathbb{N}}\).
