First countable spaces

January 29, 2022

Definitions

A topological space \((X, \T)\) is called 1st countable if and only if every point in \(X\) has a countable neighborhood basis.

Properties

Theorem 5.14: Every 2nd countable space is also 1st countable.

Theorem 5.18: In a 1st countable space, any set \(A\) with a limit point \(p\) has a sequence of points in \(A\) that converges to \(p\).

Theorem 5.19: Every 1st countable space is hereditarily 1st countable.

Examples

The standard topology on \(\R\) is 1st countable, as is the lower limit topology on \(\R\).
