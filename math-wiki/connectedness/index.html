Connectedness

March 3, 2022

Definition

A topological space \((X, \T)\) is connected if and only if \(X\) cannot be written as the disjoint union of two nonempty open sets.

Properties

Theorem 8.1 A space \(X\) being connected is equivalent to the following conditions:

  • There does not exist a continuous function \(f: X \to \Rs\) such that \(f(X) = \{0,1\}\).
  • \(X\) cannot be written as the disjoint union of two nonempty separated sets.
  • \(X\) cannot be written as the disjoint union of two nonempty closed sets.
  • The only subsets of \(X\) that are closed and open are \(X\) and the empty set.
  • For every pair of points \(p,q \in X\) and every open cover \(\{U_\alpha\}_{\alpha \in \lambda}\) of \(X\), there exists a finite subcollection \(\{U_{\alpha_1}, \dots, U_{\alpha_n}\}\) such that \(p \in U_{\alpha_1}\), \(q \in U_{\alpha_n}\), and for all \(i < n\), \(U_{\alpha_i} \cap U_{\alpha_{i+1}} \neq \emptyset\).

Theorem 8.4: If \(A,B \subset X\) are separated and \(C \subset A \cup B\) is connected, then either \(C \subset A\) or \(C \subset B\).

Theorem 8.5: Let \(\{C_\alpha\}_{\alpha \in \lambda}\) be a collection of connected subsets of a space \(X\), and let \(E\) also be a connected subset of \(X\). If \(E \cap U_{\alpha} \neq \emptyset\) for all \(\alpha \in \lambda\), then \(E \cup \left( \bigcup_{\alpha \in \lambda} C_\alpha \right)\) is connected.

Theorem 8.6: Let \(C \subset X\) be connected and \(D \subset X\) satisfies \(C \subset D \subset \overline{C}\), then \(D\) is connected as well.

Theorem 8.8: If \(C \subset X\) is connected such that \(X - C\) is the union of separated sets \(A\) and \(B\), then \(A \cup C\) and \(B \cup C\) are also connected.

Theorem 8.9: If \(f: X \to Y\) is continuous and surjective, then \(X\) being connected implies that \(Y\) is connected as well.

Theorem 8.12: If \(\{X_\alpha\}_{\alpha \in \lambda}\) is a collection of topological spaces, then their product \(\prod_{\alpha \in \lambda} X_\alpha\) with the product topology is connected if and only if \(X_\alpha\) is connected for all \(\alpha \in \lambda\).

Examples

\(\Rs\) (the real line with the standard topology) is connected.

The closure of the topologist’s sine curve is connected.

The product of countably many copies of \(\Rs\) is connected when given the product topology by Theorem 8.12 above, but is not connected when given the box topology.
