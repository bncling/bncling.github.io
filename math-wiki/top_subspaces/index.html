Subspaces in topology

July 17, 2021

Much like in other areas, a subspace is a subset that still has extra structure.

Definition

Let \((X, \T)\) be a topological space and let \(Y\) be a subset of \(X\). The collection \[\T_Y = \{ U \mid U = V \cap Y \text{ for some } V \in \T \}\] is a topology on \(Y\) called the subspace topology or the relative topology on \(Y\) inherited from \(X\). The space \((Y, \T_Y)\) is called a (topological) subspace of \((X, \T)\). If \(U \in \T_Y\) we say \(U\) is open in \(Y\).

Properties

If a set \(U\) is open in \((Y, \T_Y)\), it is not necessarily open in \((X, \T)\).

Theorem 3.28: Let \((Y, \T_Y)\) be a subspace of \((X,\T)\). A subset \(C \subset Y\) is closed in \((Y, \T_Y)\) if and only if there is a set \(D \subset X\) that is closed in \((X, \T)\) such that \(C = D \cap Y\).

Corollary 3.29: Let \((Y, \T_Y)\) be a subspace of \((X, \T)\). A subset \(C \subset Y\) is closed in \((Y, \T_Y)\) if and only if \(\overline{C} \cap Y = C\) where the closure is taken in \((X, \T)\).

Theorem 3.30: Let \((Y, \T_Y)\) be a subspace of \((X,\T)\). If \(\B\) is a basis for \(\T\), then \(\B_Y = \{B \cap Y \mid B \in \B \}\) is a basis for \(\T_Y\).

Theorems 4.19 and 4.20: Every subspace of a Hausdorff space is Hausdorff and every subspace of a regular space is regular.

Theorem 4.23: If \((Y, \T_Y)\) is a subspace of \((X, \T)\) where \(Y\) is closed in \((X, \T)\) and \((X, \T)\) is normal, then \((Y, \T_Y)\) is normal as well.

Theorem 4.25: Let \((Y, \T_Y)\) be a subspace of \((X, \T)\) and let \(A\) and \(B\) be disjoint closed sets in \((Y, \T_Y)\). Then we have that \(\overline{A} \cap B = A \cap \overline{B} = \emptyset\) where the closures are taken in \((X, \T)\). That is, \(A\) and \(B\) are separated.

Examples

The set \(Y =\) \([0,1)\) is a subset of \(\R\) and therefore it is a subspace of \(\R\) with the standard topology. The set \((-1,\frac{1}{2})\) is open in \(\Rs\), so the set \(Y \cap (-1, \frac{1}{2}) = [0, \frac{1}{2})\) is open in \((Y, \T_Y)\) even though it is not open in \(\Rs\). The complement of \([0, \frac{1}{2})\) in \(Y\) is \([ \frac{1}{2}, 1)\), which is therefore closed in \((Y, \T_Y)\) even though it is not closed in \(\Rs\).
