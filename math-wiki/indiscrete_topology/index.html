Indiscrete topology

January 9, 2022

The coarsest possible topology on a set \(X\).

Definition

Let \(X\) be a set. The indiscrete topology is \(\mathcal{T} = \{\emptyset, X\}\). The topological space \((X, \{\emptyset, X\})\) is called an indiscrete space.

Properties

By definition, the only sets in the indiscrete topology that are open are \(X\) and \(\emptyset\), and these are also the only closed sets.

Separation Properties

  • Not a \(T_1\)-space
  • Not Hausdorff
  • Regular but not a \(T_3\)-space
  • Normal but not a \(T_4\)-space

The indiscrete topology is regular and normal vacuously. Since the only nonempty closed set is the whole space \(X\), there is no point not in \(X\) or other closed set to separate from \(X\).

Countability Properties

  • Separable
  • 2nd countable
  • 1st countable
