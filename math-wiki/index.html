Discontinuities

Definition A function \(f: \mathcal{D} \to \mathbb{R}\) is discontinuous at a point \(c \in \mathcal{D}\) if and only if \(f\) is not continuous at \(c\). That is, if the limit \(\lim \limits_{x \to c} f(x)\) does not exist or is not equal to \(f(c)\).
