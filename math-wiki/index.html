Math Wiki

This is a collection of several brief explanations of various math concepts / objects I’ve come across, organized into one nice central location for my own use.

In pages with the “topology” tag, there are sometimes references to theorems, lemmas, and exercises from the book Topology Through Inquiry by Michael Starbird and Francis Su that I’ve been working with this summer. The theorems that are referenced in these pages are (usually) ones that I’ve proven (or at least tried to prove—no guarantees all the proofs work), and you can find a compilation of all the work I’ve done here.

In pages with the “linear_algebra” tag, the symbol \(\F\) usually denotes either the field \(\R\) or the field \(\C\), following the text Linear Algebra Done Right. There are also references to theorems and exercises from the book.

The collection is currently organized to have the latest updated pages at the top, but I might be looking to change that at some point. For now, you can look at an alphabetical list of all wiki entries here.

Identification spaces

Definitions Let \((X, \T)\) be a topological space and let \(X^*\) be a partition of \(X\). The identification map \(f: X \to X^*\) is the surjective map that takes each point of \(X\) to the part of the partition \(X^*\) containing it. Quotient spaces

Generalizing identification spaces. Definitions Let \((X,\T)\) be a topological spaces, let \(Y\) be a set, and let \(f: X \to Y\) be a surjection. Define the quotient topology on \(Y\) to be the topology in which \(U \subset Y\) is open if and only if \(f\inv(U)\) is open in \(X\). Given a surjective map \(f: X \to Y\) between topological spaces \(X\) and \(Y\), we call \(f\) a quotient map and \(Y\) a quotient space if and only if the topology on \(Y\) is the quotient topology with respect to \(Y\)....

The box topology

Another, weirder topology on an infinite product. Definition Let \(\{(X_\alpha, \T_\alpha)\}_{\alpha \in \lambda}\) be a (possibly infinite) collection of topological spaces. The box topology on the product \(\prod_{\alpha \in \lambda} X_\alpha\) is the topology generated by a basis consisting of sets of the form \(\prod_{\alpha \in \lambda} U_\alpha\) where \(U_\alpha\) is open in \((X_\alpha, \T_\alpha)\) for each \(\alpha\). Homeomorphisms

Definition Let \(X\) and \(Y\) be topological spaces. A function \(f: X \to Y\) is called a homeomorphism if it is bijective and both \(f\) and \(f\inv\) are continuous. When there exists a homeomorphism \(f: X \to Y\), we say the two spaces \(X\) and \(Y\) are homeomorphic or that they are topologically equivalent. Properties Theorem 7.26: Being homeomorphic is an equivalence relation on topological spaces.
