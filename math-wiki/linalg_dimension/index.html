Dimension of a vector space

July 18, 2021

Definition

The dimension of a finite dimensional vector space \(V\), denoted \(\dim V\), is the length of any basis of \(V\).

Properties

(2.18) Theorem: If \(U_1\) and \(U_2\) are subspaces of a vector space \(V\), then their sum satisfies \[\dim (U_1 + U_2) = \dim U_1 + \dim U_2 - \dim (U_1 \cap U_2).\]

Examples

For \(\F^n\), the space of lists of length \(n\), \(\dim \F^n = n\), and for \(\Poly_n (\F)\), the space of polynomials of degree \(\leq n\), \(\dim \Poly_n (\F) = n+1\).
