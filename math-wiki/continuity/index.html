Continuity

July 7, 2021

Continuous functions can be drawn without picking up your pencil.

The \(\varepsilon\)-\(\delta\), limit related definition

A function \(f: \mathcal{D} \to \mathbb{R}\) is continuous at a point \(c \in \mathcal{D}\) if and only if the limit of \(f\) at \(c\) exists and \(\lim \limits_{x \to c} f(x) = f(c)\). A function \(f: \mathcal{D} \to \mathbb{R}\) is continuous if and only if it is continuous at the point \(c\) for all \(c \in \mathcal{D}\).

Examples

  • Every rational function is continuous at every point in its domain.
  • Thomae’s function is continuous at every irrational point and discontinuous at every rational point.
