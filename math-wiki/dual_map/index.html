The dual map

October 13, 2021

Definition

Let \(V\) and \(W\) be vector spaces and let \(T \in \Lin(V, W)\) be a linear map. Then the dual map \(T': W' \to V'\) is defined by \(T' (\varphi) = \varphi \circ T\) for \(\varphi \in W'\) where \(V'\) and \(W'\) are the dual spaces of \(V\) and \(W\).

Properties

The map \(T'\) is linear since it is the composition of a linear map and a linear functional.

The map \(': \Lin(V, W) \to \Lin(W', V')\) is itself a linear map since if \(T, S \in \Lin(V, W)\) and \(a \in \F\),

  • \((T + S)' = T' + S'\);
  • \((aT)' = aT'\).

(3.101) Proposition: If \(T \in \Lin(U, V)\) and \(S \in \Lin(V, W)\), then \((ST)' = T'S'\).
