Boundaries of sets

July 7, 2021

Definition

For a set \(A\) in a topological space \((X, \mathcal{T})\), the boundary of \(A\), denoted \(\partial A\) or Bd\((A)\), is the intersection of the closures of \(A\) and its complement, that is, the set \(\partial A = \overline{X} \cap \overline{X - A}\).

Properties

Theorem 2.28: If \(A\) is a set in a topological space \((X, \mathcal{T})\), the interior of \(A\), the boundary of \(A\), and the interior of the complement of \(A\) are disjoint sets whose union is \(X\).
