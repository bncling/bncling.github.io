Connected components

March 18, 2022

Definition

Let \(p\) be a point in a topological space \((X, \T)\). The component of \(p\) in \(X\) is the union of all connected subsets of \(X\) that contain \(p\).

Properties

Theorem 8.18: Given a space \(X\), every component in \(X\) is connected, closed, and not contained in any strictly larger connected set.

Theorem 8.19: Given a space \(X\), the set of components of \(X\) is a partition of \(X\).

Lemma 8.22: If \(A\) and \(B\) are components of a compact Hausdorff space \(X\), then there exists pair of separated subsets \(H\) and \(K\) containing \(A\) and \(B\) respectively such that \(X = H \cup K\).

Theorem 8.23: If \(X\) is a compact Hausdorff space and \(X^*\) is the collection of components of \(X\), then \(X^*\) as an identification space is a compact Hausdorff space.

Theorem 8.24: If \(X\) is a compact Hausdorff space with closed subsets \(A\) and \(B\) such that no component of \(X\) intersects both, then there exists a pair of separated subsets \(H\) and \(K\) containing \(A\) and \(B\) respectively such that \(X = H \cup K\).
