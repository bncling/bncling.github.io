Internal direct products

Definition Let \(G\) be a group with subgroups \(H\) and \(K\). We say that \(G\) is the internal direct product of \(H\) and \(K\) if and only if the follow three conditions hold: \(H\) and \(K\) are normal in \(G\) (\(H, K \triangleleft G\)), \(H\) and \(K\) intersect trivially (\(H \cap K = \{e\}\)), \(G\) is the set \(HK = \{hk \mid h \in H, k \in K\}\). Properties Lemma: If \(H,K \triangleleft G\) and \(H \cap K = \{e\}\), then for all \(h \in H\) and \(k \in K\), \(hk = kh\), that is, elements in \(H\) commute with elements in \(K\)....

Noncrossing partitions

Definition Noncrossing partitions of \([n]\) are partitions of \([n]\) into parts \(\{B_1, \dots, B_k\}\) such that if \(a < b < c < d\) are elements of \([n]\) with \(a,c \in B_i\) and \(b,d \in B_j\), then \(i = j\). Otherwise, a partition is crossing. Normal subgroups

Definition A subgroup \(H\) of a group \((G,\cdot)\) is said to be normal, written \(H \triangleleft G\), if and only if its left and right cosets are equal. Order of an element in a group

Definition Let \((G, \cdot)\) be a group and let \(g \in G\). The order of \(g\), denoted \(\vert g \vert\) is the smallest positive integer \(n\) such that \(g^n = e\), the identity element of \(G\). If such an \(n\) exists, \(g\) has finite order. Otherwise, \(g\) has infinite order. Properties By Lagrange’s Theorem, the order of \(g\) always divides the order of the group, \(\vert G \vert\).
